# Creative Brief Guide

A creative brief is the foundation for effective ad concepts. It aligns your team on messaging, goals, and audience before creative execution begins.

## What is a Creative Brief?

A creative brief is a short document (1-2 pages) that outlines:
- **What** you're advertising (product/service/offer)
- **Who** you're targeting (audience segment)
- **Why** they should care (key benefit/value prop)
- **How** you'll communicate (tone, messaging, channels)
- **When** this runs (timing, urgency)

**Purpose**: Ensure everyone (marketers, designers, copywriters) is aligned before creating ads.

## Creative Brief Template

### 1. Campaign Overview
**What is this campaign?**
- Product launch, promotion, brand awareness, lead generation, etc.

**Campaign Name**: [Internal reference name]

**Example**: "Q1 Flash Sale - Spring Collection Launch"

---

### 2. Objective
**What do you want to achieve?**

Be specific and measurable:
- ❌ Vague: "Increase awareness"
- ✅ Specific: "Drive 1,000 email signups in 7 days"
- ✅ Specific: "Generate 500 product sales during flash sale weekend"
- ✅ Specific: "Book 50 product demo calls from B2B decision-makers"

**Example**: "Generate 2,000 new trial signups for Pipeline Pro in March"

---

### 3. Target Audience
**Who are you trying to reach?**

Describe your segment:
- **Demographics**: Age, location, role/job title (if B2B), income level (if B2C)
- **Behaviors**: How they currently solve this problem, media consumption habits
- **Pain Points**: What frustrates them, what problems they're trying to solve
- **Motivations**: What drives their purchase decisions (price, quality, status, convenience)

**Example**:
```
Target: B2B sales managers at 50-500 person companies

Demographics:
- Age 30-45, primarily US-based
- Job titles: Sales Manager, VP Sales, Director of Sales Ops

Behaviors:
- Currently using spreadsheets or basic CRM for pipeline tracking
- Frustrated with lack of real-time visibility
- Spending 5+ hours/week chasing pipeline updates

Pain Points:
- Can't forecast accurately
- Deals slip through cracks
- No visibility into rep activity

Motivations:
- Hit quota and earn commission
- Get promoted by showing results
- Reduce manual reporting work
```

---

### 4. Key Message
**What's the one thing you want them to remember?**

This is your core value proposition in one sentence:
- ❌ Too broad: "Our product is great"
- ✅ Specific: "Get real-time pipeline visibility in 5 minutes, no IT required"
- ✅ Specific: "Save 40% on your favorite products this weekend only"
- ✅ Specific: "The easiest way to track customer conversations"

**Example**: "Close 23% more deals with real-time pipeline visibility and AI-powered risk alerts"

---

### 5. Supporting Messages (3-5 key points)
**What evidence supports your key message?**

Bullet points highlighting:
- Key features or benefits
- Differentiators vs. competitors
- Social proof (customer count, testimonials, ratings)
- Specific outcomes or results

**Example**:
```
1. Real-time pipeline visibility - see every deal, every rep, instantly
2. AI-powered alerts - know which deals are at risk before it's too late
3. 5-minute setup - no IT required, works with your existing CRM
4. Proven results - customers close 23% more deals on average
5. Trusted by 2,000+ sales teams including TechCorp, SalesCo, and others
```

---

### 6. Tone & Voice
**How should we sound?**

Choose 2-3 tone descriptors:
- Professional, casual, playful, urgent, educational, empathetic, bold, witty, authoritative

**Example**: "Professional but approachable - like a senior sales colleague giving advice. Avoid jargon. Use data to build credibility but keep it human."

---

### 7. Call-to-Action (CTA)
**What action do you want them to take?**

Be specific:
- ❌ Vague: "Learn more"
- ✅ Specific: "Start your free 14-day trial (no credit card required)"
- ✅ Specific: "Shop the 40% off flash sale"
- ✅ Specific: "Download the free guide"
- ✅ Specific: "Book a 15-minute demo"

**Example**: "Start free 14-day trial" (primary CTA for all ads)

---

### 8. Channels
**Where will these ads run?**

- Email, SMS, Instagram, Facebook, Google Ads, LinkedIn, etc.

**Example**: "Email, SMS, and Instagram (images only)"

---

### 9. Timing & Urgency
**When does this run? Is there a deadline?**

- Launch date
- End date
- Any urgency elements (limited time, scarcity)

**Example**: "Campaign runs March 15-31. Flash sale pricing ends March 31 at midnight (create urgency in final 48 hours)"

---

### 10. Constraints or Requirements
**Are there any must-haves or must-avoids?**

- Brand guidelines (colors, fonts, logo usage)
- Legal disclaimers required
- Competitor comparisons allowed/not allowed
- Specific imagery requirements

**Example**: "Must include 'No credit card required' messaging. Avoid using competitor names. Use brand blue (#2C5F8D) for CTAs."

---

## Example: Complete Creative Brief

### Campaign: Pipeline Pro Free Trial - Q1 Growth Campaign

**1. Campaign Overview**
Product: Pipeline Pro (sales pipeline management SaaS)
Type: Free trial acquisition campaign

**2. Objective**
Generate 2,000 new trial signups in March 2026

**3. Target Audience**
B2B sales managers at 50-500 person companies, age 30-45, US-based. Currently using spreadsheets or basic CRM, frustrated with lack of real-time visibility. Motivated by hitting quota and reducing manual work.

**4. Key Message**
"Close 23% more deals with real-time pipeline visibility and AI-powered risk alerts"

**5. Supporting Messages**
- Real-time pipeline visibility across entire team
- AI alerts for at-risk deals
- 5-minute setup, no IT required
- Customers close 23% more deals on average
- Trusted by 2,000+ sales teams

**6. Tone & Voice**
Professional but approachable. Data-driven but human. Like a senior sales colleague giving advice.

**7. Call-to-Action**
"Start your free 14-day trial (no credit card required)"

**8. Channels**
Email, SMS, Instagram

**9. Timing**
March 1-31, 2026. No hard deadline but emphasize "start today" urgency.

**10. Constraints**
- Must include "No credit card required"
- Use brand blue (#2C5F8D) for CTAs
- Avoid competitor name-calling

## Common Brief Mistakes

### ❌ Too Vague
"We want to increase awareness and drive engagement with our target market."

**Problem**: No specific objective, audience, or messaging guidance.

### ❌ Too Long
5-page brief with 20 supporting messages, 10 tone descriptors, and detailed brand history.

**Problem**: Creative teams won't read it. Keep it to 1-2 pages max.

### ❌ No Clear CTA
"We want people to learn about our product and maybe sign up if they're interested."

**Problem**: Unclear what action you're optimizing for.

### ❌ Targeting Everyone
"Our audience is anyone who could benefit from our product - consumers and businesses of all sizes."

**Problem**: Can't create compelling messaging without a specific target.

### ✅ Good Brief
- 1-2 pages total
- Specific objective with numbers
- Clearly defined audience with pain points
- One key message, 3-5 supporting points
- Clear CTA
- 2-3 tone descriptors
- Channel and timing specified

---

## Using This Guide

If a user doesn't have a creative brief, ask them the 10 questions above to help them build one. Then use the completed brief to generate ad concepts aligned with their goals.
