# Quality Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to assess the quality of ad concepts and guide iterative improvement. The scoring system helps identify strengths and areas for refinement.

## Overview

**Total Possible Score**: 35 points (7 dimensions × 5 points each)

**Scoring Tiers**:
- **32-35**: Excellent - Ready to use
- **25-31**: Strong - Minor refinements needed
- **18-24**: Good - Needs iteration before launch
- **0-17**: Needs work - Major revision required

---

## Scoring Creative Directions vs. Detailed Concepts

### When to Score Creative Directions

Creative directions are **high-level strategic approaches** presented before detailed concepts are generated. Score them to help users select the most promising direction.

**What you're scoring**: Strategic fit, not execution details
- Is the direction aligned with the brief?
- Will the target segment resonate with this approach?
- Can this direction work across all target channels?
- Is it differentiated from generic approaches?

**Simplified scoring for directions**:
Focus on these 5 key dimensions (instead of all 7):
1. **Brief Alignment** (0-5) - Matches campaign goals and messaging?
2. **Segment Relevance** (0-5) - Appeals to target audience?
3. **Channel Appropriateness** (0-5) - Works across Email/SMS/Instagram?
4. **Differentiation** (0-5) - Stands out from competitors?
5. **Clarity** (0-5) - Direction is clear and understandable?

**Max score for directions**: 25 points (5 dimensions × 5 points each)

**Scoring tiers for directions**:
- **23-25**: Excellent - Strong strategic direction
- **18-22**: Strong - Good direction with minor considerations
- **13-17**: Good - Workable but needs refinement
- **0-12**: Needs work - Significant strategic gaps

### When to Score Detailed Concepts

After a creative direction is selected and you've generated detailed ad concepts (text copy + visual descriptions), use the **full 7-dimension rubric** below.

**What you're scoring**: Execution quality within the selected direction
- Copy quality, visual concept clarity, CTA effectiveness
- All 7 dimensions apply

---

## 7 Scoring Dimensions (For Detailed Concepts)

### 1. Brief Alignment (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How well the ad concept matches the stated creative brief

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Perfectly aligned - matches objective, key message, tone, and CTA exactly
- **4**: Strong alignment - captures essence with minor deviations
- **3**: Moderate alignment - some elements match, some drift from brief
- **2**: Weak alignment - significant gaps between concept and brief
- **1**: Minimal alignment - barely reflects the brief
- **0**: No alignment - completely off-brief

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: Brief says "urgent flash sale, 40% off, ends tonight" → Ad emphasizes urgency, discount, and deadline
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: Brief says "professional B2B tone" → Ad uses casual, playful language
- ❌ **Score 1**: Brief says "promote free trial" → Ad focuses on pricing and discounts

---

### 2. Segment Relevance (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How compelling the ad is to the target audience segment

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Highly relevant - speaks directly to segment's pain points and motivations
- **4**: Very relevant - clear connection to segment needs
- **3**: Moderately relevant - generic appeal, not segment-specific
- **2**: Weakly relevant - misses key segment characteristics
- **1**: Barely relevant - could apply to anyone
- **0**: Not relevant - wrong audience entirely

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: Targeting sales managers → "Stop chasing pipeline updates in Slack. Get real-time visibility instantly."
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: Targeting sales managers → "Our product is great for professionals who want to be more productive" (too generic)
- ❌ **Score 1**: Targeting B2B → Ad uses B2C language about "lifestyle" and "looking good"

---

### 3. Channel Appropriateness (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How well the concept fits the channel's best practices and constraints

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Perfect fit - respects all channel constraints, leverages channel strengths
- **4**: Strong fit - follows best practices with minor issues
- **3**: Acceptable fit - works but doesn't leverage channel optimally
- **2**: Poor fit - violates some channel best practices
- **1**: Very poor fit - multiple constraint violations
- **0**: Doesn't work - fundamentally wrong for the channel

**Channel-specific checks**:
- **Email**: Subject line 40-60 chars? Preview text 35-55 chars? Mobile-optimized HTML? Clear CTA?
- **SMS**: Under 160 chars (or 300 for multi-part)? Includes opt-out? Short URL? Mobile-friendly?
- **Instagram**: Primary text under 125 chars? Headline under 40 chars? Description under 30 chars? Visual-first? 1080x1080px spec?

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: Email subject line 52 chars, preview text 48 chars, responsive HTML, one clear CTA
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: SMS at 175 chars (splits into multi-part unnecessarily)
- ❌ **Score 1**: Instagram with 200-char primary text (cut off)

---

### 4. Clarity (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How quickly and easily the message is understood

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Crystal clear - instantly understandable, no confusion possible
- **4**: Very clear - message understood on first read
- **3**: Moderately clear - requires some thought to understand
- **2**: Somewhat unclear - confusing elements or vague language
- **1**: Very unclear - hard to understand what's being offered
- **0**: Incomprehensible - message is lost or contradictory

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: "Save 40% on all products. Use code SAVE40 at checkout. Ends tonight at midnight."
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: "Experience unprecedented value through our innovative solutions framework" (jargon-heavy, vague)
- ❌ **Score 1**: "Our thing helps you do the stuff better, maybe" (completely vague)

---

### 5. Differentiation (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How well the ad stands out from generic/competitor ads

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Highly differentiated - unique angle, memorable, clearly distinct from competitors
- **4**: Well differentiated - specific value prop that stands out
- **3**: Somewhat differentiated - mix of unique and generic elements
- **2**: Minimally differentiated - mostly generic language
- **1**: Not differentiated - could be for any brand
- **0**: Identical to competitor messaging

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: "The only sales tool that alerts you 48 hours before a deal goes cold - using AI trained on 100,000+ won deals"
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: "Save time and increase productivity with our sales software" (generic)
- ❌ **Score 1**: "Best-in-class solution for modern teams" (could be anyone)

---

### 6. Call-to-Action (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How clear, compelling, and actionable the CTA is

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Excellent CTA - clear, specific, benefit-oriented, removes friction, creates urgency
- **4**: Strong CTA - clear action, some benefit framing
- **3**: Adequate CTA - tells what to do but not why
- **2**: Weak CTA - vague or generic
- **1**: Very weak CTA - unclear what action to take
- **0**: No CTA or completely confusing

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: "Start your free 14-day trial - no credit card required" (specific, removes friction)
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: "Learn More" (generic, no benefit stated)
- ❌ **Score 1**: "Click if interested" (vague, passive)

**CTA quality checklist**:
- [ ] Action verb ("Start", "Get", "Shop", "Download")
- [ ] Specific outcome ("free trial", "40% off", "guide")
- [ ] Friction reducers ("no card required", "free", "instant access")
- [ ] Urgency (if appropriate) ("today only", "limited time")

---

### 7. Visual Concept Quality (0-5 points)

**What it measures**: How vivid, on-brand, and effective the visual description is

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Excellent visual - highly specific, on-brand, attention-grabbing, supports message
- **4**: Strong visual - clear direction, good brand fit
- **3**: Adequate visual - generic but workable
- **2**: Weak visual - vague or off-brand
- **1**: Very weak visual - barely described or doesn't support message
- **0**: No visual concept or completely wrong

**For Email/Instagram (with images)**:
- Composition clearly described?
- Color palette specified?
- Focal point identified?
- Brand elements included?
- Supports the messaging?

**For SMS (plain text)**:
- Layout clearly described?
- Formatting considerations mentioned?

**Examples**:
- ✅ **Score 5**: "Hero product shot centered on clean white background. Vibrant blue 'NEW' badge top-right. Matte black headphones at 30-degree angle. Minimal logo bottom-left. Clean, premium aesthetic."
- ⚠️ **Score 3**: "Product photo with nice colors"
- ❌ **Score 1**: "Some kind of image"

---

## How to Score

### Step 1: Score Each Dimension
Go through all 7 dimensions, assigning 0-5 points to each based on the criteria above.

### Step 2: Calculate Total
Add up all 7 scores for a total out of 35.

### Step 3: Determine Tier
- **32-35**: Excellent
- **25-31**: Strong
- **18-24**: Good
- **0-17**: Needs work

### Step 4: Identify Strengths
List dimensions that scored 4-5 points.

### Step 5: Identify Areas to Strengthen
List dimensions that scored 0-3 points.

### Step 6: Provide Recommendations
Based on low-scoring dimensions, suggest specific improvements.

### Dimension Bar Chart Guidelines

**Bar Length Calculation**:
- 5/5 points = 100% width (full green bar)
- 4/5 points = 80% width (amber bar)
- 3/5 points = 60% width (blue bar)
- 2/5 points = 40% width (orange bar)
- 1/5 points = 20% width (red bar)
- 0/5 points = 0% width (gray background only)

**Color Coding for Individual Dimensions**:
- 5 points: Green `#10b981` (excellent)
- 4 points: Amber `#f59e0b` (strong)
- 3 points: Blue `#3b82f6` (good)
- 2 points: Orange `#fb923c` (needs improvement)
- 1 point: Red `#ef4444` (weak)
- 0 points: Gray `#9ca3af` (missing)

### Accessibility Requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA)

**Color Contrast Ratios** (all meet 4.5:1 minimum):
- Excellent green `#10b981` on white: 7.2:1 ✅
- Strong amber `#f59e0b` on white: 5.2:1 ✅
- Good blue `#3b82f6` on white: 5.9:1 ✅
- Needs Work red `#ef4444` on white: 4.8:1 ✅

**Screen Reader Support**:
- Always include text score with visual bar (e.g., "Brief Alignment: 5 out of 5")
- Use semantic HTML `<details>` and `<summary>` for collapsible sections
- Provide tier label text alongside color (e.g., "33/35 (Excellent) ✅")

**Keyboard Navigation**:
- `<details>` elements are natively keyboard-accessible
- Tab through collapsible sections
- Enter/Space to expand/collapse

### When to Use Visual Score Presentation

**Always use visual dashboards for**:
- Presenting final scores for creative directions (Phase 1)
- Comparing multiple concept scores side-by-side
- Showing iteration improvements (before/after scores)
- Quality assessment summaries

**Use simple text scores for**:
- Quick inline references (e.g., "This concept scored 28/35")
- Embedded scores within other sections
- When space is constrained

## Sample Scored Concept

### Ad Concept: "Pipeline Pro Free Trial - Email"

**Subject Line**: "48 hours left: Lock in founder pricing (save $3,600/year)"
**Preview Text**: "Join 2,000+ teams who've already switched"
**Body**: Real-time pipeline visibility message with social proof and urgency
**CTA**: "Lock in Founder Pricing"

### Scoring:

1. **Brief Alignment**: 5/5 - Matches brief perfectly (B2B sales managers, free trial, professional tone, urgency)
2. **Segment Relevance**: 5/5 - Speaks directly to sales manager pain points (pipeline visibility, forecasting)
3. **Channel Appropriateness**: 5/5 - Subject 62 chars (good), preview 37 chars (good), HTML responsive, clear CTA
4. **Clarity**: 5/5 - Crystal clear offer and benefit
5. **Differentiation**: 4/5 - "Real-time pipeline visibility" and "23% more deals" are specific, but could be more unique
6. **Call-to-Action**: 5/5 - "Lock in Founder Pricing" is specific, benefit-oriented, urgent
7. **Visual Concept Quality**: 4/5 - Clean gradient header, professional aesthetic, but could be more distinctive

**Total Score: 33/35 (Excellent)**

### Strengths:
✅ Perfect brief alignment (5/5)
✅ Highly relevant to segment (5/5)
✅ Channel best practices followed (5/5)
✅ Crystal clear messaging (5/5)
✅ Excellent CTA (5/5)

### Areas to Strengthen:
⚠️ Differentiation (4/5) - Could emphasize unique AI-powered features more
⚠️ Visual concept (4/5) - Professional but could be more memorable

### Recommendations:
- Consider emphasizing "AI-powered deal risk alerts" as a unique differentiator
- Explore more distinctive visual treatments (e.g., subtle data visualization in header)

## Using Scores to Guide Iteration

Iteration can happen at **three levels**: Creative Direction, Text Concepts, or HTML Previews. Use scores to guide refinement at each level.

---

### Iteration Level 1: Creative Direction Scoring (0-25 points)

**If direction scores 23-25 (Excellent)**:
**Action**: Proceed to generating detailed concepts with this direction.

**Example feedback**:
> "Excellent strategic direction! This scored 24/25 with strong brief alignment and segment relevance. Let's generate detailed ad concepts using this 'Benefit-Focused' approach."

**If direction scores 18-22 (Strong)**:
**Action**: Usable but consider refining the direction first, or generate concepts and see how it develops.

**Example feedback**:
> "Strong direction (20/25). Channel appropriateness could be stronger (3/5) - this might work better for email than SMS due to complexity. Would you like to refine the direction, or shall we generate concepts and adapt per channel?"

**If direction scores 13-17 (Good)**:
**Action**: Refine the direction before proceeding to concepts.

**Example feedback**:
> "This direction needs refinement (15/25). Segment relevance is weak (2/5) - the 'Innovation-Forward' angle may not resonate with risk-averse enterprise buyers. Try a direction that emphasizes proven results and reliability instead."

**If direction scores 0-12 (Needs Work)**:
**Action**: Generate new direction options; this one has significant strategic gaps.

**Example feedback**:
> "This direction isn't quite right (11/25). Let's explore different strategic angles - would urgency-driven or social proof-driven approaches align better with your flash sale brief?"

---

### Iteration Level 2: Text Concept Scoring (0-35 points)

**If concept scores 32-35 (Excellent)**:
**Action**: Ready for HTML preview generation. Concept is strong.

**Example feedback**:
> "Excellent! This concept scores 33/35. It's ready for HTML mockups. The copy is compelling and the visual concept is vivid. Shall I generate the HTML preview?"

**If concept scores 25-31 (Strong)**:
**Action**: Minor refinements recommended before generating HTML.

**Example feedback**:
> "Strong concept (28/35). Two areas to strengthen: Differentiation (3/5) - the message is a bit generic vs. competitors. Try emphasizing your unique AI alerts feature. Visual concept (3/5) - add more specific color choices and composition details. Want me to refine, or proceed to HTML?"

**If concept scores 18-24 (Good)**:
**Action**: Iteration needed on copy or visual concept before HTML.

**Example feedback**:
> "Good start (22/35). Three areas need work: Segment relevance (2/5) - this language feels too generic for sales managers. Use their specific pain points like 'stop chasing Slack updates'. Differentiation (3/5) - clarify what makes you different. CTA (3/5) - 'Learn More' is vague; try 'Start Free Trial' instead."

**If concept scores 0-17 (Needs Work)**:
**Action**: Major revision required. Regenerate text concept.

**Example feedback**:
> "This concept needs significant work (14/35). Let's refocus: Brief alignment (2/5) - the brief asked for urgency but there's none here. Clarity (2/5) - the message is confusing. Channel appropriateness (2/5) - subject line is 85 characters (too long). Let's revisit the selected direction and try a new concept."

---

### Iteration Level 3: HTML Preview Refinement

**When**: User has confirmed text concepts and you've generated HTML mockups

**Common HTML refinements** (no scoring needed):
- Color adjustments ("Make the header blue instead of purple")
- Layout tweaks ("Add more spacing around the CTA button")
- Typography changes ("Use a bolder headline font")
- Visual design polish ("Increase contrast on the Instagram image background")

**No re-scoring needed** for HTML-only changes unless they significantly alter the concept's strategic approach.

---

## Best Practices for Scoring

✅ **Be objective** - Use the criteria, not personal preference
✅ **Be specific** - "Differentiation is weak because..." not just "needs work"
✅ **Focus on highest-impact improvements** - Don't nitpick 4s when there are 1s
✅ **Offer concrete suggestions** - "Try emphasizing X" not "improve this"
✅ **Balance criticism with praise** - Highlight strengths before weaknesses

❌ **Don't be vague** - "This is good" isn't helpful
❌ **Don't score everything 3** - Use the full 0-5 range
❌ **Don't let one dimension bias others** - Score each independently
❌ **Don't forget context** - A 3 in one dimension might be fine if others are 5s

---

## Summary

The quality scoring rubric helps you:
- **Objectively assess** ad concepts against clear criteria
- **Identify strengths** to reinforce in future iterations
- **Pinpoint weaknesses** to address with specific improvements
- **Guide iteration** toward higher-quality concepts
- **Communicate feedback** clearly to users

Use this rubric after generating concepts to help users understand quality and decide whether to iterate or proceed to production.
